Legg Mason may acquire Clarion Partners


p { margin-bottom: 0.25cm; line-height: 120%; } The international asset management firm Legg Mason is in negotiations to acquire a majority stake in the investment company Clarion Partners, specialised in real estate, the news agency Bloomberg reports. The company is valued at about USD850m. According to the terms under dicussion, Legg Mason would acquire a stake of 80%, while the current management of Clarion would retain a 20% stake. In November last year, the news agency Reuters reported that the private equity firm Lightyear Capital was planning to sell its majority stake in Clarion for a total of about USD800m. At the time, Legg Mason appeared already to have been showing an interest in Clarion, Reuters then stated.
