US hedge funds threaten to leave Europe


P { margin-bottom: 0.08in; } Several major US hedge funds have called off plans to set up shop in Europe, or are planning to leave the region within a few weeks of the AIFM directive, which will seriously handicap the ability of non-European managers to sell their products directly to investors, comes into force, Financial Times Fund Management reports. The prospect of an exodus of US asset management firms is a potential source of concern for European pension funds. 10% of hedge funds based in the United States currently have an office in Europe, according to Preqin.
