France - Daniel Bouton resigns as chairman of Société Générale


Daniel Bouton, chairman of the board of directors at Société Générale, has announced his resignation ?from 6 May,? in a statement issued by the bank this morning. The board of directors will elect a new chairman on this date, Bouton says. ?Repeated attacks against me personally, in France, in the past 15 months have affected me, and more importantly, they run the risk of damaging a bank with 163,000 employees,? says the director, who admits, ?like all directors, [of having] certainly made mistakes.? He argues that ?the strategy adopted has put Société Générale among the best-positioned banks in the Euro zone.? In an interview published this morning in Le Figaro, Bouton states that he will be leaving the bank with ?zero? severance indemnities.
