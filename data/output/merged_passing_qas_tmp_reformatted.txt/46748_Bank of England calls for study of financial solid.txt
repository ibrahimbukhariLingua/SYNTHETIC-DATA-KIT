Bank of England calls for study of financial solidity of asset management firms


A head at the Bank of England has suggested in a column in the Financial Times that the asset management sector may be subjected to stress tests. The financial policy committee at the central bank has now announced measures of this nature.At a quarterly meeting held on 24 March, the board resolved to collect information from asset management firms about their liquidity management strategies in scenarios including normal activity as well as crisis situations, according to a statement from the Bank of England dated 26 March. This will allow the Bank to better evaluate the ability of asset management firms to keep up with considerable or massive redemption demands within a short period
