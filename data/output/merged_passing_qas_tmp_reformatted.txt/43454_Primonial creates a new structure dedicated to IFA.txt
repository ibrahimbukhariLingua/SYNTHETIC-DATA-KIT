Primonial creates a new structure dedicated to IFAs


p { margin-bottom: 0.25cm; line-height: 120%; } The Primonial group on 8 September announced the creation of Primonial Partenaires, a new structure dedicated to independent financial advisers (GCPI). The new entity, born of the merger of the “Patrimmofi” and “Partenariats Externes” units, is one of the largest supplies of products and services for CGPIs in France, with inflows of EUR600m in 2015, and over EUR2bn in assets under management as of the end of 2014. As part of the creation, Stefan de Quelen is appointed as CEO of Primonial Partenaires. De Quelen, 43, began his career in 1993 at Barclays Bank. In 1995, he was appointed as deputy director of Robeco bank, in charge of developing an IFA product range. In 2000, he become director of sales at Fidelity, and then in 2008, became director of external distribution at Natixis Asset Management. Since 2013, he led the External Partners teams at Primonial, responsible for sales to CGPIs.
