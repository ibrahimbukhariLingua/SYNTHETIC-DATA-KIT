Paul Manduca joins RateSetter


p { margin-bottom: 0.1in; line-height: 120%; } Paul Manduca, who was CEO of Deutsche Asset Management from 2002 to 2005, has joined the UK fintech RateSetter as non-executive chairman. Manduca, who is currently chairman of Prudential, will join the board of directors on 1 June and will become chairman on 17 July, succeeding Alan Hughes. Manduca has served in a variety of senior positions in the asset management sector. He participated in the creation of Threadneedle Asset Management, and was CEO of Rothschild Asset Management and Deutsche AM. RateSetter, founded in 2010, is a peer-to-peer lending network. The firm in 2015 obtained the financial support of Woodford and Artemis.
