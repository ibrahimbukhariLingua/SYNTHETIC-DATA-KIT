Carlyle and Warburg Pincus acquire ratings agency DBRS


p { margin-bottom: 0.1in; line-height: 120%; } The private equity firms Carlyle and Warburg Pincus have announced the joint acquisition of the Canadian ratings agency DBRS, in partnership with a consortium of individual investors based in Canada, including Walter Schroeder, founder of DBRS, and the management of DBRS. The operation, for an undisclosed amount, is expected to be completed in first quarter 2015. In a statement, Carlyle says that the headquarters of DBRS will remain in Toronto. The agency, which also has offices in New York, Chicago and London, provides ratings of more than 1,000 different companies and vehicles.
