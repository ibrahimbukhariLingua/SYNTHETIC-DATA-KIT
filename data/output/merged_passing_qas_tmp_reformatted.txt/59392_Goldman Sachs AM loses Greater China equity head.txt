Goldman Sachs AM loses Greater China equity head


Alina Chiew, head of Greater China equity at Goldman Sachs Asset Management, depart last month after some 11 years at the firm, Asian Investor can reveal. She had taken up the role in 2010 and her licence at GSAM expired on july 15. A soure said she was heading to a large sovereign wealth fund. Guan Shao-Ping, a member of the China equity investment team, has been promoted to replace Chiew as lead China portfolio manager. He had previously focused on consumer and industrials sector coverage and also retains that responsibility. Guan joined the team in Hong Kong in 2007.Furthermore, Sherry Zhu joined in june from US funds Vanguard as an analyst on the China institutional team based in Hong Kong. GSAM declined to comment on the hire.
