Pension fund PGGM lays off 200


p { margin-bottom: 0.1in; line-height: 120%; } The Netherlands-based pension fund PGGM, which has EUR178bn in assets under management as of the end of September, will lay off at least 200, equivalent to 15% of its personell, as part of a restructuring programme to reduce costs by EUR50m by 2017, Reuters reports. The restructuring comes as a response to recent changes in the pension market in the Netherlands, which has confronted major difficulties in offering high returns and meeting increased governmental requirements since the financial crisis. At present, PGGM has about 1,300 employees.
