BNY Mellon IM launches a mobility innovation fund


@page { size: 8.27in 11.69in; margin: 0.79in }p { margin-bottom: 0.1in; line-height: 115%; background: transparent }@page { size: 8.27in 11.69in; margin: 0.79in }p { margin-bottom: 0.1in; line-height: 115%; background: transparent }BNY Mellon Investment Management, which has USD1.8trn in assets under management, is launching the BNY Mellon Mobility Innovation Fund.The themed long-only fund of international equities will be managed by an investment team led by Sean Ftizgibbon at the Boston Company, a commercial brand from BNY Mellon Asset Management North America.The fund will replicate the first version of the strategy, launched earlier in the year in Asia, whose assets total USD3bn.The fund aims to generate long-term capital growth, by investing in companies of all market cap sizes which are causing profound changes to the way in which we use, power and control our means of transportation,‚Äù a statement explains. The fund will invest in companies in various sectors involved in the theme of mobility revolution. These exposures include innovative software solutions and new data infrastructure capacities required to assist the growth of autonomous vehicles, advanced driving assistance (ADAS) systems necessary for road safety, a rise in demand for electrical vehicles resulting from changes in the regulatory framework to promote green energy, and increasing use of ride-sharing applications.The fund will be available to investors based in France, Denmark, Germany, Italy, Switzerland, the Netherlands, Spain, and the United Kingdom, as well as to private banks in Singapore and Hong Kong, among others.
