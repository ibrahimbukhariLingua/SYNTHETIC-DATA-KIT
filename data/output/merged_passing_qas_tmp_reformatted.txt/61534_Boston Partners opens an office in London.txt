Boston Partners opens an office in London


Brexit will not slow down international investors. The US asset management firm Boston Partners, which has USD90.6bn in assets under management, on 3 May announced that it is opening an office in London to accelerate its development serving European clients. The new entity is led by a two-member team composed of Joshua Jones and William Pawson. Jones, a portfolio manager for international products, will direct the investment team, while Pawson, a senior product specialist, will oversee distribution. The new office will work to assist the activities of Boston Partners in Europe, which now represent USD14bn in assets advised and managed in UCITS format, as well as USD2.2bn in managed accounts for nine European institutional clients.
