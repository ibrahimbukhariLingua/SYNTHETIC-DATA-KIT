Schroders adds to team in Frankfurt


p { margin-bottom: 0.25cm; line-height: 120%; } The British asset management firm Schroders has recruited Daniel Lösche as investment strategist for its office in Germany. Lösche will be based in Frankfurt, and will be responsible for local capital market strategies, analysis of competitors, and product development. Before joining Schroders, Lösche worked at BHF Bank, where he served most recently as asset allocation adviser for the private banking division. Before that, he worked at DJE Group and at Main First Bank.
