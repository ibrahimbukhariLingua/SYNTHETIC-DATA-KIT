Money market funds: SEC proposes its rules


P { margin-bottom: 0.08in; } The Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) on Wednesday presented its proposed regulations for US money market funds, Agefi reports. They apply to premium type institutional money market funds. They will have two options, which are not exclusive of one another: either adopt a variable net asset value rather than a constant net asset value (USD1 per share), and to set up liquidity commissions and barriers to redemption in the event of stress. The suspension of redemptions may last up to 30 days. The proposals are subject to consultation for a period of 90 days.
