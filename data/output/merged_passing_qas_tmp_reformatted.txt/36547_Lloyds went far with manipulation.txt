Lloyds went far with manipulation


p { margin-bottom: 0.1in; line-height: 120%; } Lloyds bank on 28 July agreed to pay USD370m in fines to end investigations by the US and UK authorities into manipulation of inter-bank lending rates, Agefi reports. In addition to Libor, it now emerges that traders attempted to defraud the emergency liquidity system set up by the Bank of England at the peak of the crisis.
