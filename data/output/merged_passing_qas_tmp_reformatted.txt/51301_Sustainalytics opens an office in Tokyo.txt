Sustainalytics opens an office in Tokyo


p { margin-bottom: 0.25cm; line-height: 120%; } The research agency Sustainalytics, a specialist in sustainable development and corporate governance, has announced the opening of an office in Tokyo, Japan. The new location will allow the firm to better serve its institutional clients in the fast-growing Japanese market in the area of corporate governance in particular. Loic Dujardin and Yumi Fujita, currently based in Singapore, will move to Tokyo to lead the local Japanese research team, which will be reinforced from this autumn, the firm says in a statement. Research at Sustainalytics into corproate governance and the ESG approach covers over 6,500 companies worldwide, and its research in controversial cases has highlighted ESG risks at over 12,000 companies.
