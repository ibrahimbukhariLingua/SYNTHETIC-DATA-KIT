Edmond de Rothschild AM appoints a new head of Chinese equities


p { margin-bottom: 0.25cm; line-height: 120%; } Edmond de Rothschild Asset Management (EDRAM) has appointed Weiwei Li to the position of head of Chinese equity management. Li will be based in Hong Kong, and will report to Thomas Gerhardt, director of emerging market equity and commodity management. She replaces Xing Hu, who left the group on 1 March “to develop a new professional career,” the asset management firm says in a statement. Li, who has 18 years of experience in the financial industry in London and Hong Kong, joined Edmond de Rothschild Asset Management (Hong Kong) in 2011 as a senior portfolio manager, after working as an Asia specialist at Altima Partners from 2007 to 2011. She previously worked in London as a manager specialised in continental China at Fabien Pictet & Partners, and developed her experience at several international financial institutitons, such as ABN Amro and Invesco.
